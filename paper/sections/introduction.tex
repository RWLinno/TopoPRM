\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Mathematical reasoning is among the most challenging tasks for large
language models (LLMs), requiring not merely correct final answers but
logically sound, step-by-step derivations where each conclusion follows
from its premises~\citep{ElKishky2024OpenAIOS,Chen2025TowardsRE}.
Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards
(RLVR)~\citep{deepseekr1,shao2024deepseekmath} has emerged as a powerful
paradigm for improving reasoning: by rewarding models for producing
correct answers, RLVR fine-tunes policies to explore and refine their
reasoning strategies without requiring human preference annotations.

However, current RLVR methods rely almost exclusively on outcome-level
supervision, treating the reasoning process as an opaque black box. This
leaves the \emph{structure} of reasoning entirely unsupervised, giving
rise to several well-documented failure modes.

\paragraph{Limitations of outcome-only RLVR.}
We identify three systematic shortcomings:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Outcome hacking.} Models learn to shortcut to correct
    answers without constructing logically sound derivations. The reward
    signal provides no gradient for improving the quality of the reasoning
    trace itself~\citep{Denison2024SycophancyTS}.

    \item \textbf{Structural incoherence.} Without explicit modelling of
    inter-step dependencies, reasoning traces often contain orphan
    conclusions (claims without supporting premises), circular references,
    and inconsistent logical direction.

    \item \textbf{Long-chain redundancy.} Without any length or
    coherence penalty, RLVR-trained models tend to produce verbose,
    repetitive reasoning chains that waste inference budget and often
    degrade accuracy through self-contradictions~\citep{reasoning_compression_2025}.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{TopoPRM: topology-aware process rewards.}
Inspired by the structured cognitive mechanisms of human
reasoning~\citep{George2021ClonestructuredGR}, we observe that
mathematical derivations inherently possess a topological structure:
each reasoning step depends on a subset of previous steps, forming a
directed acyclic graph (DAG). We propose to leverage this structure as a
source of verifiable process rewards, rather than relying on learned
reward models or human annotations.

Concretely, we propose \textbf{TopoPRM}, a composite reward framework
that supplements the outcome reward with two topology-aware process
signals. Given a model-generated reasoning trace, we extract its implicit
reasoning DAG by parsing inter-step dependency patterns. A
\emph{topological structure reward} $R_{\mathrm{topo}}$ then evaluates
whether this DAG satisfies basic structural properties---acyclicity,
connectivity, and directional consistency. Concurrently, a
\emph{continuity reward} $R_{\mathrm{cont}}$ measures whether each
step can be derived from preceding context, penalising unsupported
logical jumps. Both signals are computed programmatically and require
no learned reward model.

To prevent the auxiliary process rewards from causing reward hacking
(where models optimise structural quality at the expense of
correctness), we introduce \emph{Stratified Clipping Advantage
Estimation} (SCAE), which hierarchically separates correct and incorrect
completions during advantage computation. Finally, we apply
reverse-KL distillation to compress the improved reasoning behaviour
from a 32B teacher into smaller models for efficient deployment.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Our main contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
    \item We propose TopoPRM, a topology-aware verifiable process
    reward that evaluates the structural quality of reasoning DAGs
    without requiring a learned reward model.

    \item We design Stratified Clipping Advantage Estimation (SCAE)
    to hierarchically prioritise correctness over structural quality,
    preventing reward hacking from auxiliary rewards.

    \item We introduce a composite GRPO framework combining outcome,
    topological structure, continuity, and format rewards, all computed
    programmatically from model outputs.

    \item We conduct extensive experiments on mathematical reasoning
    benchmarks, demonstrating that TopoPRM improves accuracy while
    producing more structurally coherent and concise reasoning traces.
\end{enumerate}
