\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented TopoPRM, a topology-aware process reward framework for
reinforcement fine-tuning of mathematical reasoning models. By augmenting
standard outcome rewards with topological structure and continuity
signals---both fully verifiable and requiring no learned reward
models---TopoPRM improves reasoning accuracy while producing more concise
and structurally coherent reasoning chains. Our Stratified Clipping
Advantage Estimation (SCAE) method effectively prevents reward hacking
from auxiliary process rewards by hierarchically prioritising answer
correctness. The reverse-KL distillation pipeline further compresses
these improvements into smaller student models for efficient deployment.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our DAG extraction relies on rule-based parsing of inter-step references,
which may miss implicit dependencies in free-form reasoning. The
continuity reward uses regex-based expression matching rather than
semantic understanding, which limits its sensitivity to subtle logical
gaps. Our evaluation focuses on mathematical reasoning; applicability to
other structured reasoning domains (e.g., code generation, scientific
reasoning) is left for future work.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Several directions merit investigation: (i)~learning the reward weights
adaptively during training, (ii)~extending the topological reward to
handle probabilistic dependency structures, (iii)~combining TopoPRM with
learned process reward models for complementary supervision, and
(iv)~applying TopoPRM to multi-modal reasoning tasks.
