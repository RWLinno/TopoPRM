\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\paragraph{Setup.}
We use Qwen3-32B~\citep{qwen3} as the base model. Training is conducted
using MS-Swift~\citep{msswift} with LoRA (rank 64, alpha 128). SFT uses
5000 samples mixing Chinese mathematical critique data (637 samples) with
public English datasets (NuminaMath-CoT, MetaMathQA). GRPO uses 637
Chinese mathematical critique prompts with TopoPRM composite rewards. All
experiments run on 8$\times$ NVIDIA L20X GPUs (143 GB each). We evaluate
on MATH500, GSM8K~\citep{cobbe2021gsm8k}, CMATH (Chinese math), and
GaoKao (Chinese college entrance exam). Additional training details and
hyperparameters are provided in Appendix~\ref{app:details}.

\subsection{Main Results}
\label{sec:main_results}

\input{tables/main_results}

Table~\ref{tab:main_results} presents the main results across four
benchmarks. TopoPRM (GRPO with full composite reward) consistently
outperforms the SFT baseline and outcome-only GRPO across all benchmarks.
The improvements are particularly pronounced on more challenging
benchmarks (GaoKao), suggesting that topology-aware process rewards
provide stronger guidance for complex multi-step reasoning.

\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

\input{tables/ablation_reward}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation_reward} shows the contribution of each reward
component. Removing the topological structure reward leads to the largest
accuracy drop, confirming that DAG-level structural verification is the
most impactful process signal. Removing the continuity reward also
degrades performance, though to a lesser extent. The format reward
contributes primarily to output consistency rather than reasoning quality.

\subsection{Length Analysis}
\label{sec:length_analysis}

\input{tables/ablation_length}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation_length} analyses the accuracy-length trade-off.
TopoPRM produces shorter reasoning traces than outcome-only GRPO while
maintaining higher accuracy, demonstrating that topology-aware rewards
naturally encourage more concise reasoning by penalising redundant
branches and orphan conclusions.

\subsection{Structural Quality}
\label{sec:structural_quality}

\input{tables/structural_metrics}

Table~\ref{tab:structural_metrics} reports structural metrics of the
generated reasoning traces across training stages. GRPO with TopoPRM
significantly improves DAG acyclicity, reduces orphan conclusions, and
increases directional consistency compared to SFT and outcome-only
baselines.

\subsection{Reasoning Compression}
\label{sec:compression_results}

\input{tables/compression}

Table~\ref{tab:compression} presents distillation results. The 14B
student retains the majority of the 32B teacher's accuracy while the 7B
student achieves competitive performance at significantly reduced
inference cost, validating the effectiveness of reverse-KL distillation
for transferring topologically coherent reasoning patterns.
