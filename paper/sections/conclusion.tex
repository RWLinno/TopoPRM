\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented TopoPRM, a topology-aware process reward framework for
reinforcement fine-tuning of mathematical critique models. By augmenting
standard outcome rewards with topological structure and continuity
signals---both fully verifiable and requiring no learned reward
models---TopoPRM improves critique accuracy while producing more concise
and structurally coherent reasoning chains. Our reverse-KL distillation
pipeline further compresses these improvements into smaller student
models suitable for efficient deployment.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our DAG extraction relies on rule-based parsing of structured step
annotations, which assumes the model has been trained to produce such
annotations. Extending TopoPRM to free-form reasoning without explicit
step markers remains an open challenge. Additionally, the continuity
reward uses a frozen embedding model whose quality directly affects the
coherence signal; domain-specific embeddings may yield further gains.
Our evaluation focuses on mathematical critique; applicability to other
structured reasoning domains (e.g., code review, scientific peer review)
is left for future work.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Several directions merit further investigation: (i)~learning the reward
weights $w_o, w_t, w_c, w_f$ adaptively during training rather than
fixing them as hyperparameters, (ii)~extending the topological reward
to handle uncertainty and probabilistic dependency structures,
(iii)~applying TopoPRM to multi-turn dialogue settings where the
reasoning graph spans multiple conversational turns, and (iv)~exploring
the combination of TopoPRM with learned process reward models for
complementary supervision.
